    Verse 1

    This verse describes an idyllic scene where Jimmy is “nibblin’ on sponge cake” and “watchin’ the sun bake,” representing the epitome of leisure and relaxation. He observes tourists sunbathing (“covered with oil”) and enjoys simple pleasures like playing guitar and smelling food cooking.

    Chorus 1 of “Margaritaville”

    “Margaritaville” refers to a state of mind rather than an actual place, symbolizing an attitude of not worrying too much about life’s troubles. The singer is “searchin’ for [his] lost shaker of salt,” a metaphor for missing elements or issues in life that aren’t particularly serious. Initially, he believes “it’s nobody’s fault,” indicating a carefree view.

    “Wastin’ away again in Margaritaville
    Searchin’ for my lost shaker of salt
    Some people claim that there’s a woman to blame
    Now I think, hell, it could be my fault”

    Verse 2

    Despite enjoying his time, the singer admits he doesn’t know why he stayed the entire season. He has “nothing to show” except a “brand new tattoo,” a symbol of spontaneity and perhaps mild recklessness.

    Verse 3

    Jimmy encounters a minor setback—his flip-flop breaks, and he cuts his heel—but he takes it in stride. The “booze in the blender” serves as another escape, a “frozen concoction” that helps him cope.

    Chorus and Outro

    Finally, he fully acknowledges that he’s responsible for his own situation, saying “it’s my own damn fault.”

    In Conclusion

    “Margaritaville” encapsulates a blend of the following:

    • escapism
    • personal responsibility
    • the acceptance of life’s minor troubles

    It represents the idea of finding peace and enjoyment even when things aren’t perfect, advocating a balanced, laid-back approach to life’s challenges. This is a mentality that we encourage (:

    When was “Margaritaville” released?

    This officially came out vis ABC Records on January 20th, 1977. It is one of the tracks found on Jimmy’s seventh studio album, which is titled “Changes in Latitudes, Changes in Attitudes”.

    Did “Margaritaville” chart?

    Yes. It was a number 1 in Canada. On the US’ Billboard Adult Contemporary chart, it also made it to number 1. On the Hot 100, it attained a peak position of 8.

    Who has covered this song?

    • Alan Jackson included a version of this classic in his 1999 album “Under the Influence”. He also performed it with Jimmy Buffett.
    • The Chipmunks included a cover of “Margaritaville” in their 2009 album “Summer Time!”

    Credits

    Jimmy Buffett receives sole writing credit for “Margaritaville”. On the production side, American music producer Norbert Putnam is the track’s sole credited producer.
