    Donny Van de Beek Bio

    Van de Beek began his career as a boy with the local Veensche Boys’ academy where his father, André, once played. In August 2014, he joined the Ajax youth academy signing a three-year contract, and was assigned to the D-team. He progressed further through the academy and featured in the A1 team Supercup victory against the Feyenoord A1 team. On 27th January 2015, he signed a contract extension, to keep him at the club until mid-2018.

    Van de Beek made his debut for Jong Ajax (the Ajax reserve team) in the Eerste Divisie against Sparta Rotterdam in January 2015. At the end of the 2014/2015 season, he won the AFC Ajax Talent of the Future award.

    In November 2015, Van de Beek was called to the senior team for a UEFA Europa League match against Scottish club Celtic. He made his European debut in the match, which Ajax won 2–1. He was officially promoted to the first team on 16 February. Media reports suggested that German club Bayern Munich and the Spanish club, Barcelona, were interested in signing him. By the end of the 2015/2016 season, he cemented his position with the first team, and was also playing regularly for the Ajax under-19 team in the UEFA Youth League. 

    In the 20162017 season, Ajax progressed to the Europa League Final, but lost to Manchester United. During the 2017/2018 season, Van de Beek established himself as a key player in Ajax’s starting eleven. In July 2018, he rejected an offer made by Italian club Roma citing that “Ajax is the right place for his development”.

    Following the 2018/2019 campaign, in which Ajax reached the semi-finals of the UEFA Champions League, Van de Beek was listed among the 30-man shortlist for the Ballon d’Or. With Ajax, he won the Eredivisie title, KNVB Cup and Johan Cruyff Shield.

    In August 2020, Van de Beek signed a five-year contract with English Premier League club Manchester United for the transfer fee of £35 million plus £5 million in add-ons. Van de Beek chose to wear the number 34 jersey as a tribute to former Ajax teammate Abdelhak Nouri, who collapsed during a friendly in 2017 and went into an induced coma. Van de Beek had little involvement during his first season at Manchester United, starting only four Premier League matches.

    In his second year, he was not started in the Premier League at all, although he did get a few starts in the Champions League and the EFL Cup. On 31st January 2022, Van de Beek was loaned out to Premier League club Everton until the end of the 2021/2022 season.

    On 14th November 2017, Van de Beek made his senior international debut against Romania in a friendly match. He appeared in both games in the 2019 UEFA Nations League Finals as a substitute, as the Netherlands defeated England 3–1 but lost to Portugal in the final 1–0. He scored his first international goal on 14 October 2020 in a 1–1 away draw against Italy in the UEFA Nations League.
