    Biography of Roald Dahl

    In Cardiff, Wales, on September 13, 1916, Roald Dahl was born to wealthy Norwegian immigrants Harald and Sofie. Alfhild, Else, Asta, and Astri were his four sisters. At the age of seven, Astri died of appendicitis, and a few weeks later, Dahl’s father passed away from pneumonia.

    Dahl, whose native tongue is Norwegian, attended the Church of Norway, the nation’s official Lutheran church. He first went to St. Peter’s boarding school in Weston, Somerset, England after attending the Cathedral School in Llandaff, Wales.

    Dahl started attending Repton School in Derbyshire when he was 13 years old. He was subjected to physical punishment by the headmaster and a culture of ritualistic cruelty among the students there, which plagued him. He played football, cricket, and golf during his time in school, and he also became interested in writing and photography.

    After finishing school, Dahl traveled across the Atlantic and went on a trip in Canada’s Newfoundland with the Public Schools Exploring Society. He afterwards joined the Shell Petroleum Corporation, which put him in charge of positions in Tanganyika and Kenya, where he lived in a luxurious Shell House.
