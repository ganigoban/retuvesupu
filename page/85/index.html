Can piriformis syndrome be seen on MRI?

02 Sep 2024, 00:00

With its exquisite depiction of soft tissues, MRI aids in the diagnosis of piriformis syndrome by demonstrating normal and abnormal anatomy of the piriformis muscle and sciatic nerve, and helping to exclude other possible causes of external sciatic nerve entrapment at the level of the greater sciatic foramen. What kind of MRI do they do for piriformis syndrome? MRI. MRI is the only modality that can adequately visualize the region. The radiographic appearance depends on the cause, and often no abnormality is noted.
